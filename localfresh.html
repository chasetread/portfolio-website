LocalFresh began as a web based recipe app focusing on fixing common frustrations users have. One single realization while interviewing potential users helped me to realize the space between recipe app users and their ingredients, and that is how the true identity of the project came to be.

LocalFresh was created with Figma, and Lyssna was used for User Testing. Below you'll find my creative process laid out in chronological order.

My Process

Problem Statement
In the last 25 years, there's been a growing emphasis on healthy eating and food traceability, but current recipe web apps often fall short, resulting in frustrating user experiences.

Solution
By simplifying recipes and providing connection to local ingredients and farmers, our targeted users will not only have better health outcomes but also gain community.

Product Overview
LocalFresh is a responsive web-based recipe app. It connects users with their local community. It offers quality recipes and a simple interface. Users can find fresh ingredients locally from the recipe page. It is designed to be a comprehensive solution for users of all ages and culinary skill levels.

Design Process
The Design Process began with the principles of Lean UX in mind. A competitive analysis of similar brands was conducted. Following, User Research interviews guided my decision making in the planning stage. Below you will see the development plan I followed in creating LocalFresh. This consisted of a Discovery, Concepting, and Prototyping and User Testing stage.

Research

Discovery: Competitive Analysis
Key Objectives, Overall Strategy, Market Advantage, Marketing Profile, SWAT Analysis, and UX Analysis were all determined for two competing brands, Kitchen Stories and Forks Over Knives. Above you'll see a summary of my competitive analysis. The key difference found was that neither Kitchen Stories or Forks over Knives allow users to find local ingredients from the recipe page.


Questions for Interviewees

  • How would you describe your ability as an at cook? How often do you cook at home using a recipe app or website?
  • Where do you usually shop for ingredients when grocery shopping? How far is this from your home (in miles)?
  • Why do you cook at home (as opposed to ordering from a restaurant)?
  • How often do you use farmer's markets to source ingredients? Why? What is important for you in choosing a recipe website or app?
  • What features would you implement or improvements would you make to a recipe website if given the chance?
  • Would you have a preference for locally sourced ingredients vs. ingredients from a chain grocery? What are the contributing factors to your preference?


Interview Synthesis
 All respondents mentioned the cost-effectiveness of home cooking, expressed a preference for local ingredients, and found farmers markets inconvenient; moreover, they described recipe web pages as difficult to navigate and cluttered.

Conclusion

LocalFresh would bridge a crucial gap in many communities between our farmers and app users. In addition the simple, easy to follow recipes and social features give users a unique reason to choose our app over many competitors. Looking forward to next steps I will add size breakpoints for all screens of LocalFresh to take advantage of increasing screen space.
