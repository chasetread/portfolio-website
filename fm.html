FriendMe - Case Study

FriendMe is my third built mobile app. I received a project brief that was rather simple in stating that the objective was “To design the iOS and Android version of a mobile app that solves a specific user problem.”. The following guidelines were given:

  • Must follow iOS and Material Design guidelines
  • Native mobile design patterns used
  • Clearly defined typographic hierarchy
  • Appropriate use of color
  • Appropriate use of haptic and sound feedback for interactions
  • User gestures annotated
  • UI must be intuitive
  • UI must be intuitive
  • Consistent UI design applied across all screens
  • The app is designed for accessibility

My Process

Problem Statement
Apps prioritizing human connection are very rare, and those that do exist prioritize romantic relationships.

Solution
FriendMe will facilitate the friend making process for those searching for connection in our increasingly online world and make friendships based on common interests the central focus.

Key Features

  • New users will be able to sign in and create a customizable profile.
  • User can search for individuals with common interests via swiping.
  • Users be able to message matches who have mutual interests.

Deliverables Requested

  • Project proposal
  • 1 to 3 user flows
  • 8 to 12 low-fidelity wireframes for iOS
  • 8 to 12 low-fidelity wireframes for Android
  • 8 to 12 digitized wireframes for iOS
  • 8 to 12 digitized wireframes for Android
  • Prototype of iOS UI
  • Prototype of Android UI
  • User feedback from 5 test participants

Research

User Flow
The 3 key features highlighted earlier will be achieved by the user using the interconnected User Flow. Each flow was initially created separately then integrated together to create the wireframes for FriendMe.

Ideation

Low Fidelity
Low fidelity screens were created in Figma first for iOS then for Android.

Sound and Haptic Feedback

EX 1: One set of touch feedback demonstrations. It was important to add a “swiping” feature to the profile photos as it falls in line with user expectations and allows for an intuitive user experience.

EX 2: The swiping screen would benefit from haptic feedback, particularly the Like and Dislike buttons could provide a light vibration to confirm your choice and to know that the feature is working correctly. This would be implemented in both versions.

EX 3: It will be important to notify the user of any new messages received. A sound and vibration like a typical messenger would accompany a new message from your match pool along with a drop down alert. The sounds would resemble each operating system, with a simple sound being used for iOS and a slightly richer and more unique sound being used for Material Design.

Design and Prototype

User Testing
Prototypes with high fidelity screens for both iOS and Material Design were tested with 4 iOS and 3 Android users being accounted for. The pain points from this exercise allowed me to make the final tweaks I needed for the current final build of FriendMe.

Takeaways from Feedback

  • Add navigation on the match screen, including an "X" and a back arrow.
  • Ensure the back button takes you back to the match screen, not messages once in messages.
  • Resize the profile picture in the messaging UI (iPhone).
  • Address design issues with the Super Like button, emphasizing and/or labeling it for better understanding (lightning bolt is ambiguous).
  • Resolve clashes with the red button on the swipe screen.
  • Explore ways to differentiate the messaging screen.
  • Make back buttons functional on all screens.
  • Consider adding a separate section for matches, possibly with a gear icon above the match component.
  • Reevaluate the design of text fields and labels, particularly for Android.
  • Assess if the use of teal is overused and adjust as needed.
  • Enhance the prominence of the icon bar at the bottom of the screen, particularly for Android.
  • FriendMe resembles other apps in the market. To differentiate it, I will focus on design, excluding the 'Superlike' button and ensuring consistent branding and logo placement on key screens.

Final Design Mockups

Using the feedback and all the aforementioned planning, the final design for Roadrunner was created. First in iOS, second in Material Design.

Conclusion

Creating FriendMe was a such a great learning experience. I feel very confident in accomplishing the goal of creating an experience conducive to friend making in the modern world. Learning the intricacies of designing for iOS and Android also taught me many small differences in design principals can lead to a largely unique experience for the user.

If I could start the process over I would love to create even more features and quirks to differentiate FriendMe from other apps on the market and implement them into my early stages of planning. More updates will be provided in the future beginning with profile editing screens to better express yourself!
